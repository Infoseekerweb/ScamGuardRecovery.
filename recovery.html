Step-by-step guidance if you've been scammed

‚è∞ Time is Critical!

If you were scammed in the last 24-72 hours, follow the IMMEDIATE ACTIONS below. The faster you act, the better your chances.

üö® Immediate Actions (First 72 Hours)

NOW

Stop All Contact

  • Block the scammer on all platforms (phone, email, social media)
  • Do NOT send any more money (even if they claim it's to "unlock" your funds)
  • Do NOT try to negotiate or recover funds yourself
  • Save everything - don't delete conversations or emails
‚ö†Ô∏è Scammers often ask for "fees," "taxes," or "processing charges" to release your money. This is part of the scam!
0-24 HRS

Contact Financial Institutions

Call immediately - some transactions can be reversed if caught early!

Credit Card

  • Call the fraud department number on back of card
  • Report unauthorized charges
  • Request chargeback investigation
  • Freeze or cancel the card

‚úÖ Recovery Rate: 60-90%

Bank Transfer

  • Call your bank's fraud hotline immediately
  • Request transaction reversal if within 24 hours
  • File fraud report
  • Change online banking credentials

‚ö†Ô∏è Recovery Rate: 30-50% if caught early

Wire Transfer

  • Western Union: 1-800-325-6000
  • MoneyGram: 1-800-666-3947
  • Ria: 1-877-443-1399
  • Report fraud immediately
  • May be able to stop transfer if not picked up

‚ö†Ô∏è Recovery Rate: 10-20%

Payment Apps

  • PayPal: resolution.paypal.com
  • Venmo: help.venmo.com
  • Cash App: cash.app/help
  • Zelle: Contact your bank (Zelle is bank-to-bank)
  • Report unauthorized transaction
  • File dispute if applicable

‚ö†Ô∏è Varies - some have buyer protection

Cryptocurrency

  • Document wallet addresses involved
  • Report to exchange if funds went through one
  • File police report (needed for potential restitution)
  • Report to CFTC.gov for crypto fraud

‚ùå Recovery Rate: 5-10% (very difficult)

Gift Cards

  • Contact the gift card company
  • Provide card numbers and receipts
  • File police report
  • They may be able to track if cards haven't been used

‚ùå Recovery Rate: <5% (nearly impossible)

24-72 HRS

Report to Authorities

Official reports are crucial - even if money isn't recovered, you need documentation!

1. Local Police

  • File an official police report
  • Get the case/report number
  • Provide all evidence and documentation

Why: Required for insurance claims, credit disputes, and potential restitution

2. FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)

  • Website: www.ic3.gov
  • File detailed complaint online
  • Include all transaction details
  • Attach evidence if possible

Why: Federal database helps track and catch scammers across state lines

3. Federal Trade Commission (FTC)

  • Website: www.ftc.gov/complaint
  • File consumer complaint
  • Report scam details
  • Share contact information used

Why: Helps FTC build cases against scam operations

Additional Reports (Based on Scam Type):

  • Investment/Securities: SEC.gov/tcr
  • Cryptocurrency: CFTC.gov
  • Email/Domain: abuse@[provider].com
  • Social Media: Report directly to platform
  • IRS Impersonation: treasury.gov/tigta

ONGOING

Secure Your Accounts & Monitor

üîê Change Security Credentials:

  • Update passwords on all financial accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication everywhere possible
  • Update security questions
  • Change email password if it was compromised

üëÅÔ∏è Monitor for Identity Theft:

  • Check credit reports (free at annualcreditreport.com)
  • Set up fraud alerts with credit bureaus
  • Consider credit freeze if personal info was stolen
  • Watch for new accounts opened in your name
  • Monitor bank statements for unauthorized charges

üìß Watch for Follow-Up Scams:

  • Scammers sell victim lists to other scammers
  • Beware of "recovery services" contacting you
  • Anyone promising guaranteed recovery for a fee is scamming again
  • Report new scam attempts immediately

üìã What Happens Next?

Realistic Expectations

Be prepared for the following timeline:

  • Investigation: Can take weeks to months
  • Recovery: Depends on payment method and speed of reporting
  • Legal Action: May take years, if successful at all
  • Credit Card Disputes: Usually resolved in 30-90 days

üí≠ Important: Most scam money is not recovered. However, reporting is still crucial to help catch scammers and prevent future victims.

Beware Recovery Scams!

üö® You may be targeted again with "recovery services":

Red Flags:

  • ‚ùå Guarantees they can get your money back
  • ‚ùå Requires upfront payment or "processing fees"
  • ‚ùå Claims to have "special connections" or "hacking abilities"
  • ‚ùå Contacts you unsolicited (found your report online)
  • ‚ùå Pressures you to act quickly

‚ö†Ô∏è TRUTH: Legitimate recovery involves law enforcement, your bank, or licensed attorneys. No one can guarantee recovery, and real professionals don't charge upfront fees for contingent recovery.

‚öñÔ∏è Legal Options

When to Consider an Attorney

Legal action may be worth pursuing if:

  • Loss exceeds $10,000
  • Scammer is identifiable and has assets
  • You have solid documentation
  • Multiple victims are involved (class action potential)

Finding Legal Help:

  • State Bar Association referrals
  • Attorneys specializing in fraud/consumer protection
  • Free consultations (many offer this)
  • Contingency basis (no fee unless they win)

Small Claims Court

For smaller amounts (limits vary by state, usually $5,000-$10,000):

  • No attorney required
  • Lower filing fees
  • Faster process
  • But: Requires knowing defendant's real identity and location

‚ö†Ô∏è Most scammers use fake identities and are overseas, making this option impractical.

Restitution Programs

If scammers are caught and convicted:

  • Courts may order restitution to victims
  • Funds distributed among verified victims
  • Process can take years
  • Recovery is often partial, not full

üí° Your police report and FTC/IC3 complaints help you qualify for future restitution programs.

üíö Emotional Recovery & Support

Being scammed is not just a financial loss - it's emotionally traumatic. Many victims experience shame, anger, anxiety, and loss of trust. These feelings are normal and valid.

Remember:

  • ‚úÖ It's not your fault - scammers are professional criminals
  • ‚úÖ You're not alone - millions are scammed every year
  • ‚úÖ Being cautious going forward is smart, not paranoid
  • ‚úÖ Your experience can help protect others

Healthy Coping:

  • Talk to trusted friends/family
  • Join support groups (online or local)
  • Consider counseling if trauma is severe
  • Focus on what you can control going forward
  • Channel emotions into helping others (report, warn, educate)

Moving Forward:

  • Learn from the experience without dwelling on it
  • Rebuild trust gradually
  • Use protective measures (see Safety Tips)
  • Remember you're wiser now
